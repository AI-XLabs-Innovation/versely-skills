# Platform Guide — Versely Social

Best practices, format requirements, and tips for each supported platform.

## Instagram

**Best formats:**
- Images: 1080x1080 (1:1), 1080x1350 (4:5), 1080x1920 (9:16 for Reels/Stories)
- Videos: MP4, 9:16 vertical for Reels, max 90 seconds for Reels
- Carousels: Up to 20 images in a single post

**Caption tips:**
- Max 2,200 characters, but first 125 characters visible in feed
- Use 20-30 relevant hashtags (put in comment or at end)
- Line breaks work — use them for readability
- Include a CTA (call-to-action) early

**Best posting times:** Tue-Fri 9am-12pm, Wed 11am (peak engagement)

## TikTok

**Best formats:**
- Videos: 9:16 vertical, 1080x1920, 15-60 seconds optimal
- TikTok automatically adds trending music to slideshow/carousel posts

**Caption tips:**
- Max 2,200 characters
- Use 3-5 trending hashtags
- Keep first line as a hook — "Watch till the end!"
- Shorter is better — 150 chars optimal

**Best posting times:** Tue-Thu 10am-12pm, Fri 5pm

**Note:** TikTok auto-adds trending music to carousel posts created through the API.

## YouTube

**Best formats:**
- Videos: 16:9 horizontal, 1080p+, no strict duration limit
- Shorts: 9:16 vertical, under 60 seconds

**Caption tips:**
- First line becomes the video title if not set separately
- Use full descriptions — YouTube indexes text for search
- Include timestamps, links, and hashtags (max 15)
- Add keywords in first 2 lines for SEO

**Best posting times:** Thu-Fri 12pm-3pm, Sat 9am-11am

## Twitter / X

**Best formats:**
- Images: 1200x675 (16:9) or 1080x1080 (1:1)
- Videos: MP4, up to 2:20, max 512MB
- GIFs supported

**Caption tips:**
- Max 280 characters (strict limit)
- Front-load the key message
- Use 1-2 hashtags only — more looks spammy
- Threads for longer content

**Best posting times:** Mon-Fri 8am-10am, Wed 12pm

## Facebook

**Best formats:**
- Images: 1200x630 (link shares) or 1080x1080 (feed)
- Videos: MP4, 16:9 or 1:1, optimal under 3 minutes

**Caption tips:**
- No strict character limit, but 40-80 chars get most engagement
- Questions and CTAs drive interaction
- Tag relevant pages when appropriate
- Use emojis sparingly for visual breaks

**Best posting times:** Wed 11am-1pm, Thu-Fri 1pm-3pm

## LinkedIn

**Best formats:**
- Images: 1200x627 (landscape) or 1080x1080 (square)
- Videos: MP4, under 10 minutes, subtitles recommended
- Documents/PDFs: Carousel-style document posts perform well

**Caption tips:**
- Professional tone — avoid slang and excessive emojis
- Max 3,000 characters, but 150-300 words optimal
- Start with a hook — first 2 lines visible before "see more"
- Use bullet points and line breaks
- 3-5 relevant hashtags

**Best posting times:** Tue-Thu 8am-10am, Wed 12pm

## Pinterest

**Best formats:**
- Images: 1000x1500 (2:3 vertical) — tall pins perform best
- Idea Pins: Multiple images/videos as a slideshow

**Caption tips:**
- Include keywords for SEO — Pinterest is a search engine
- 100-500 characters in description
- Link to source/product page
- Use clear, descriptive titles (100 chars max)

**Best posting times:** Sat 8pm-11pm, Fri 3pm

## Bluesky

**Best formats:**
- Images: Up to 4 images per post
- No video support via API yet (may change)

**Caption tips:**
- Max 300 characters (strict limit)
- Similar to early Twitter — casual, authentic tone
- Hashtags work but less established convention
- Growing platform — good for early adopter content

**Best posting times:** Mon-Fri 9am-12pm (US time zones)

## Threads

**Best formats:**
- Images: 1080x1080 (1:1) or 1080x1350 (4:5)
- Videos: Under 5 minutes, 9:16 vertical preferred

**Caption tips:**
- Max 500 characters
- Casual, conversational tone (like Instagram's text companion)
- Short posts with personality perform best
- Reply to your own thread for longer content

**Best posting times:** Similar to Instagram — Tue-Fri 9am-12pm

---

## General Best Practices

1. **Always preview content** — Check image dimensions match platform requirements before posting
2. **Caption length matters** — Shorter for Twitter/TikTok, longer for LinkedIn/YouTube
3. **Media quality** — Use at least 1080p for all platforms
4. **Hashtag strategy** — Many for Instagram, few for Twitter, keyword-focused for Pinterest
5. **Timing** — Schedule posts during peak engagement windows for each platform
6. **Cross-posting** — Same content can work across platforms, but tailor caption length and tone
7. **Carousel advantage** — Instagram carousels get 3x more engagement than single posts
8. **Vertical video** — 9:16 format works on Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and Threads
